Most small retailers don't have a sales problem. They have a restock rhythm problem.

Who this guide is for

This guide is for business owners and operations teams planning reliable bulk moves across Lagos neighborhoods and traffic corridors.

When restocking is random, three things happen quickly: stockouts, emergency purchases, and margin leakage.

Build a weekly restock rhythm

Ready to apply this?

Turn this guide into a real move plan

Sphere operations can help you execute this flow in Lagos with the right vehicle and route timing.

Request a driver Talk to operations

Use a fixed weekly structure:

  • Monday: fast movers review
  • Tuesday: supplier confirmation
  • Wednesday: main pickup and transfer
  • Friday: top-up for weekend demand

Consistency beats improvisation.

Separate products by urgency

Create three tiers:

  • Tier A: daily fast sellers (must never stock out)
  • Tier B: steady sellers (review twice weekly)
  • Tier C: slow sellers (monthly or promotional restock)

Move Tier A on predictable windows, not "when we notice."

Use route batching

If your suppliers are in nearby zones, batch pickups in one route.

Example:

  • Supplier cluster on mainland
  • Drop to one micro-hub
  • Final movement to your store

This reduces per-trip friction and keeps operations calmer.

Keep your request notes clean

Drivers work better with clear instructions:

  • carton count
  • fragile/keep-upright items
  • pickup contact name
  • gate access notes
  • unloading support availability

The clearer your request, the better your bids.

Track your restock KPI set

You do not need complex software to improve.

Track weekly:

  • stockout events
  • emergency trip count
  • average restock turnaround
  • logistics spend as % of revenue

If stockouts drop while spend stays stable, your system is improving.

Mistakes that hurt SME growth

  • Restocking only when shelves are empty
  • No item prioritization
  • One-person logistics dependency
  • No documented process for assistants

If your process depends on one person remembering everything, it will fail under pressure.

Simple weekly operating template

  1. 30-min Monday stock review
  2. Supplier list finalized same day
  3. Mid-week core transfer
  4. Friday demand top-up
  5. Saturday metrics review

This alone can stabilize move operations in under a month.

Move checklist

  • Group inventory or equipment by priority and fragility
  • Confirm the right vehicle type for total load volume
  • Share destination access notes and receiver contact
  • Keep a move log for timing, cost, and completion quality

Frequently asked questions

Which Sphere vehicle option is best for this kind of move in Lagos?

Use compact car for light, low-volume transfers, van for bulky mixed household or business items, and medium truck for full-load movement or high-volume stock.

How early should I request a driver for a bulk move in Lagos?

For better driver matching and smoother execution, request a driver at least 24 to 48 hours ahead, especially when your move has tight access windows or multiple heavy items.

Can Sphere support recurring business movements?

Yes. Businesses can run repeat movement patterns with standardized load notes, route timing preferences, and clear receiving contacts to improve consistency over time.
