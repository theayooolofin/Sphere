Most move conflicts are not fraud. They are communication failures under pressure.

Who this guide is for

This guide is for customers and drivers who need cleaner execution and safer movement decisions across Lagos, Abuja, and Ibadan.

When a trip starts going wrong, people react emotionally and things worsen fast. This guide gives both customers and drivers a process that works.

The 5-minute reset rule

Ready to apply this?

Turn this guide into a real move plan

Sphere operations can help you execute this flow in Lagos, Abuja, and Ibadan with the right vehicle and route timing.

Request a driver Talk to operations

If there is a serious disagreement:

  1. Pause movement safely
  2. Restate current facts only
  3. Confirm expected outcome
  4. Document timestamp and status

This lowers tension and avoids impulsive decisions.

For customers: what to do first

  • Stay specific: item count, current location, expected ETA
  • Avoid personal accusations in early stage
  • Confirm if issue is delay, handling, or destination access
  • Keep records in one chat thread

Clear communication gets faster resolution than emotional escalation.

For drivers: what to do first

  • Provide location and clear status update
  • Explain delay cause and revised ETA
  • Confirm load condition if concern is damage
  • Ask for destination reconfirmation if unclear

Silence is usually interpreted as negligence.

Common conflict scenarios and responses

Scenario A: late arrival

  • Share revised ETA
  • Explain bottleneck clearly
  • Offer realistic next action

Scenario B: item concern at drop-off

  • Pause handover
  • Check item with customer present
  • Document condition
  • Escalate through support channel if needed

Scenario C: wrong destination details

  • Reconfirm full address
  • Share additional distance implication
  • Continue only after both parties agree

What not to do

  • Threaten cancellation without process
  • Move off-platform during active issue
  • Use vague updates ("coming soon", "almost there") repeatedly
  • Ignore calls/messages for long periods

These behaviors increase dispute severity.

A better closure process

At move completion:

  • Confirm item handover
  • Confirm issue resolved or escalated
  • Close with short recap message

Professional closure protects reputation on both sides.

Why this matters

Trust compounds in logistics. A market where both customers and drivers follow process gets faster, safer, and more profitable over time.

Move checklist

  • Confirm route conditions and move window before request
  • Label fragile or high-risk items clearly
  • Keep both pickup and drop-off contacts available
  • Document move completion with quick handover checks

Frequently asked questions

Which Sphere vehicle option is best for this kind of move in Nigeria?

Use compact car for light, low-volume transfers, van for bulky mixed household or business items, and medium truck for full-load movement or high-volume stock.

How early should I request a driver for a bulk move in Nigeria?

For better driver matching and smoother execution, request a driver at least 24 to 48 hours ahead, especially when your move has tight access windows or multiple heavy items.

Can Sphere support recurring business movements?

Yes. Businesses can run repeat movement patterns with standardized load notes, route timing preferences, and clear receiving contacts to improve consistency over time.
