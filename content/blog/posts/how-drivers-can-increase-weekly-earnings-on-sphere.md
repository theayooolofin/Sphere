Many drivers focus only on trip volume. The bigger opportunity is **trip quality plus consistency**. Higher earnings usually come from choosing better requests, reducing downtime, and maintaining strong customer ratings.

This guide gives a field-tested structure drivers can use weekly.

![Driver preparing a move van route inside city traffic](assets/blog/bulk-cargo-city-34066009.jpg)

## Step 1: Optimize your acceptance window

Your first hour online shapes the rest of your day. Prioritize jobs with:

- clear pickup and drop-off details
- realistic loading requirements
- acceptable distance-to-payout ratio

Avoid unclear requests that create long call cycles before movement begins.

## Step 2: Improve your profile trust signals

Customers and dispatch teams respond faster to drivers with reliable profiles. Keep these updated:

- clear profile photo
- active phone number
- complete vehicle details
- clean communication history

Trust reduces negotiation friction and improves booking speed.

## Step 3: Reduce idle gaps between jobs

Idle time quietly kills weekly earnings. Use these tactics:

- end completed trips close to high-request zones when possible
- come online before peak demand windows
- avoid long off-app pauses during active hours

A driver with moderate rates and low idle time often out-earns a high-rate driver with long inactive gaps.

## Step 4: Handle pickups like a professional operator

Consistent pickup execution creates repeat demand. At pickup:

- confirm item count quickly
- confirm fragile or high-value handling notes
- verify destination landmarks
- set realistic movement expectations

Customers remember calm, precise drivers and are more likely to rebook.

## Step 5: Protect your ratings with communication discipline

If there is delay, communicate before customers ask. Use short updates:

- arrival updates
- loading status
- delay reason with revised ETA

Clear updates reduce complaints and preserve ratings.

## Step 6: Price with long-term thinking

Undercutting every trip can increase fatigue and reduce net weekly value. Instead, evaluate:

- traffic intensity
- route complexity
- loading effort
- time block impact on your next job

Your goal is profitable flow, not just trip count.

## Step 7: Track weekly performance metrics

At the end of each week, review:

- online hours
- completed jobs
- acceptance-to-completion ratio
- total payout and average payout per hour

Simple weekly tracking helps you improve systematically, not emotionally.

## Advanced growth moves

- Study customer demand categories and peak timings
- Improve packaging guidance at pickup to reduce damage claims
- Keep your vehicle clean and ready for photo verification

If you are still refining request selection, read this first:

- [How to choose the right truck size for moving in Lagos](https://www.sphere.ng/blog/how-to-choose-the-right-truck-size-for-moving-in-lagos/)

## Driver consistency checklist

- Start with high-clarity trips
- Minimize idle time between jobs
- Communicate proactively
- Review weekly metrics
- Protect trust and ratings

This playbook helps drivers build a more stable weekly earning pattern instead of random good days.
