Who this guide is for

This guide is for business owners and operations teams planning reliable bulk moves across Lagos, Abuja, and Ibadan.

Customers remember two things: move speed and communication quality. If both fail, repeat purchase drops.

Rule 1: never dispatch without full address validation

Ready to apply this?

Before handover, confirm:

  • full name
  • active phone number
  • exact drop-off landmark
  • preferred move window

"Send location" alone is not enough.

Rule 2: use dispatch batches

Group same-axis orders instead of one-by-one dispatch chaos.

  • Mainland cluster
  • Island cluster
  • Urgent single order lane

Batching reduces errors and improves daily throughput.

Rule 3: separate payment status clearly

Tag every order:

  • paid
  • part-paid
  • pay-on-completion

This prevents handover disputes and accounting confusion.

Rule 4: package for movement, not shelf display

Pretty packaging is good, but move-safe packaging is better.

  • seal liquids well
  • protect fragile items
  • keep invoice inside
  • attach external order ID label

A clean packaging process reduces customer complaints fast.

Rule 5: send proactive customer updates

Update buyer on:

  • dispatch time
  • driver en route
  • move completed

Silence creates anxiety and unnecessary support messages.

Rule 6: keep a failed-move protocol

When a move fails, follow a process:

  1. call customer twice
  2. log reason
  3. reschedule or return stock
  4. update customer channel immediately

No protocol means profit leaks.

Rule 7: track weekly fulfillment metrics

Minimum dashboard:

  • orders dispatched
  • successful first-attempt moves
  • failed move reasons
  • average dispatch-to-move completion time

What gets measured gets improved.

Move checklist

  • Group inventory or equipment by priority and fragility
  • Confirm the right vehicle type for total load volume
  • Share destination access notes and receiver contact
  • Keep a move log for timing, cost, and completion quality

Frequently asked questions

Which Sphere vehicle option is best for this kind of move in Nigeria?

Use compact car for light, low-volume transfers, van for bulky mixed household or business items, and medium truck for full-load movement or high-volume stock.

How early should I request a driver for a bulk move in Nigeria?

For better driver matching and smoother execution, request a driver at least 24 to 48 hours ahead, especially when your move has tight access windows or multiple heavy items.

Can Sphere support recurring business movements?

Yes. Businesses can run repeat movement patterns with standardized load notes, route timing preferences, and clear receiving contacts to improve consistency over time.
